Undergraduate Biology Student

I am an undergraduate student majoring in Biology at University of California, Riverside. I am currently enrolled in my last quarter before graduating with a Bachelor of Science degree for Biology. I would describe myself as very detail-oriented, hardworking and responsible. I enjoy making sure things are organized and running smoothly. I aim to be in the health field to have a positive impact on others and assist those in need. It will allow me to use my interest in science, and further gain experience in aspects that are related to the anatomy of the body.

Through my experience at UCR I have taken multiple lab courses that have taught me how to use different equipment like the rotovap, pipettes and separatory funnel. Experiencing and working on labs from those courses helped me gain patience and knowledge of how to prepare for different experiments . I saw how important it is to have exact measurements, to be aware of the materials I was using and to know what product or outcome I wanted by the end of the lab.

This year I got into a research lab on campus that studies mutualism. Specifically the graduate student I worked with researched how incline, weather and seasons affected Nitrogen levels in plants. In the lab I would take the leaf samples she collected in colorado last summer and grind it. I used a mortar and pestle to grind the leaf samples until they were broken down into a powder like form. We did this so that we can use a machine to test for a specific nitrogen isotope. Depending on which sample exhibited the nitrogen isotope we were looking for could tell us what affects the nitrogen levels related to the environment a plant grows in. I really enjoyed working in this lab unfortunately it was only for a short period due to the pandemic and not being able to be on campus.
